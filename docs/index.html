What is the Paint To Hide game?

Paint To Hide! is a Roblox hide and seek game where hiders paint themselves into the map instead of crouching behind cover. You pick a wall, a floor, or a prop, then paint yourself to match that surface and blend into the map — choose a pose and hold still while seekers sweep. Every hider a seeker catches joins the seeker team, so late rounds turn into a many-on-few hunt. The formula traces back to MECCHA CHAMELEON, the Steam game where you paint a plain white body to mimic the stage.

How to play, in short

Paint, pose, survive the timer — or catch everyone before it runs out. Rounds are short and replayable, and the full guide covers round flow, controls, and the catch-and-convert rule.

Free cam for hiders (Shift + P)

Hiders get a free camera on Shift + P — it is on the game's own feature list. Detach the camera and inspect your paint job from the seeker's angle before you commit to a spot.

Private servers with free admin panels

Private servers come with a free admin panel, listed as a built-in perk on the official page. A private round with friends costs nothing extra to moderate.

FAQ

Is Paint To Hide the game with the SUMMER! code?

No. Codes sites list SUMMER! for Paint and Hide, a separate Roblox game with its own developer. Paint To Hide! by Chabungus X Cache Flow has no verified codes as of July 4, 2026, and codes never transfer between the two games.

Who made Paint To Hide on Roblox?

Chabungus X Cache Flow, the Roblox group behind the game, released it on May 19, 2026. In its first seven weeks it passed 50 million visits and 75,000 favorites, with concurrent players around 35,000 to 40,000 on a typical day.

Can you get banned for abusing the paint system?

Yes. The official description warns that paint-system abusers will be banned and tells players to report abuse to Roblox. Camouflage tricks within the rules are fine; exploiting paint mechanics to grief rounds is what the warning targets.
